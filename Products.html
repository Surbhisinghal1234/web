Building Wires

The most common material for building wires due to its superior conductivity, flexibility, and reliability. Copper wires are less prone to corrosion and are highly durable. While less conductive than copper, aluminum is lighter and less expensive. It is often used in large cables for service entrance and feeders. Special care must be taken to prevent oxidation and ensure proper connections.

Bunched Wire

Bunched wire is more flexible than solid wire, making it ideal for applications where the wire must bend and twist without breaking. The multiple strands provide resilience against physical stress and vibration, enhancing the wire's longevity.The surface area of bunched wire is greater than that of a solid wire, which can improve electrical conductivity slightly and reduce skin effect in high-frequency applications.

Annealed Wire

Annealed wire is a type of wire that has undergone an annealing process, a heat treatment that softens the metal, enhancing its ductility and reducing its hardness. This process involves heating the wire to a specific temperature and then slowly cooling it, which alters its physical and mechanical properties, making it more pliable and easier to work with.: Annealed wire is softer compared to other types of wire, making it easier to handle and manipulate.

Hard Drawn Wire

Hard drawn wire is a type of steel wire that has been cold drawn through a die to achieve a specific diameter and enhanced mechanical properties. This process, known as work hardening, increases the wire's tensile strength and hardness by deforming the metal's structure without the application of heat. The cold drawing process significantly increases the wire's tensile strength, making it suitable for applications that require strong and durable wire.

Earthing Wire

Earthing wire, also known as grounding wire, is an essential component in electrical systems designed to safely dissipate electrical currents into the earth. This provides a reference potential for the electrical system and helps protect against electrical shocks, surges, and potential fire hazards.Earthing wires are typically made of materials with excellent electrical conductivity, such as copper or aluminum, to ensure efficient grounding.

Superior Conductivity

Copper wire's conductivity is unmatched. It efficiently transmits electricity, minimizing energy loss. This makes it ideal for various applications.

Exceptional Durability

Copper wire is highly resilient and corrosion-resistant, ensuring long-lasting performance even in harsh conditions.

Thermal Efficiency

Copper wire dissipates heat well, preventing overheating and enhancing component lifespan.

Cost-Effectiveness

Despite its premium performance, copper wire remains cost-effective due to its long lifespan and low maintenance requirements.
